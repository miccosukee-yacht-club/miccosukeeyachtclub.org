The Club maintains a set of International Code of Signals flags sufficient to spell most short words and all of the letters in MICCOSUKEE, which is an unusual coincidence and has not gone unnoticed.

The question of how to fly them has arisen with some regularity, owing to the fact that several members have opinions on the matter and not all of those opinions are correct.

## Dressing the Ship

Full dressing — flying signal flags from stem to stern over the masthead — is reserved for the following occasions:

- Founders' Day (the date of the Club's constitution)
- The opening cruise of each season
- Any occasion the Commodore judges to warrant it, which is final and not subject to appeal

The flags are flown bow-to-stern in the standard NATO dress order. They are not flown in any arrangement that spells a word, profane or otherwise, visible from the public boat ramp.

## Proper Order

When dressing, the sequence alternates: pennant, flag, pennant, flag. This produces a visually balanced string. The Club's current inventory includes the letters A through Z with the exception of Q and X, which no one has yet required, and the numeral pennants 0 through 9.

> Flag B (Bravo) — "I am taking on, discharging, or carrying dangerous goods" — is not to be flown unless the vessel is actually doing so. The Club has no current plans to carry dangerous goods, though the matter has not been formally voted upon.

## Storage

The flags are stored in the dock box, sorted by type, rolled rather than folded. A flag that has been folded incorrectly is a flag that will be untangled at the precise moment it is wanted. The dock box combination is distributed to full members only, in person.
