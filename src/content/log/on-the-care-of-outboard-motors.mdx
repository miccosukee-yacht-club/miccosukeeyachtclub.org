The Club's operating rules on engine maintenance are few and direct. This entry sets them down in one place so that they may be consulted, cited, and — most importantly — followed.

## The Flushing Protocol

The *M.V. Porkchop Express* is powered by a Mercury 150 four-stroke outboard. Lake Miccosukee is a freshwater body, but the bilge returns sediment and organic matter to the cooling passages after sustained operation at low speed. The following procedure is to be performed after each outing exceeding two hours, or any time the throttle has been held below 1500 RPM for more than thirty minutes.

1. Secure the vessel to the dock.
2. Connect a fresh-water hose to the flush fitting on the starboard lower unit — not the ear muffs, which are for trailered engines.
3. Start the engine. Confirm water flow from the telltale.
4. Run at idle for five minutes.
5. Increase to 1500 RPM for two minutes.
6. Return to idle. Shut down. Disconnect.

This procedure is recorded in the manufacturer's documentation and is not a matter of debate.

## A Note on Oil

The engine takes Mercury Full Synthetic 4-Stroke. The capacity is 2.8 quarts. Check the dipstick before every third outing, not every outing — excessive removal of the dipstick introduces more opportunity for contamination than it prevents.

> The Club's engine will not fail from lack of attention to this protocol. It will fail from inattention. These are different things.

## Schedule

The impeller is to be replaced every two seasons, regardless of apparent condition. The lower-unit gear oil is changed annually. Both procedures are performed by Tallahassee Marine Services, not by members acting on confidence alone.
